What is slang?

Slang is an everchanging group of words and expressions that isn’t part of “standard language”. It is as ancient as language itself, yet it is generally considered below that standard.

There have been some recent developments in slang, though: it is spreading faster than ever, due to social media allowing spread without personal contact (eg. hanging out with friends) and leading to more informal communication.

Slang is usually used by groups of people who are in a political minority or have reason to hide things from others. Usually the slang originates in a smaller subculture such as gay ballroom culture (slay, eat) or incels (mogging, mewing) before spreading to larger groups – and this point is where you’ll start to take notice.

But why do teenagers use slang? Well, it mainly helps with maintaining connections within the in-group. It can be used to identify who’s part of it, and create tighter bonds within it. Slang also gets used to express informality or emotions, and to challenge authority.
